Unanswered Questions

Below is a list of questions I wish someone would research and answer for me. I don't have enough Internet or physical clout to get people fulfill this request, but anyone who finds these questions interesting is welcome to steal them or email me with an answer. Who knows, these questions might spark an interesting discussion.

And the obligatory "Let Me Google That For You".

  • How often are nutrition facts reevaluated? What is the acceptable tolerance of the data?
  • Why do we crack jokes? What is the etymology of this term?
  • How do people with advertisements or business information on their cars prepare their cars to be sold? Do they get another paint job over the business paint? Do they sell it as is? Do they never sell?
  • Why is economic growth necessary? Every resource on economics I've ever read, every business books, even this article I really like assumes growth to be the end-all-be-all.
  • How much of a construction site is cleaned before the pretty, normal-human-use pieces are put in? How do you protect a construction site from rain and other weather phenomena, like tornadoes or hurricanes?
  • How do you maintain airflow in underground structures? For example, parking garages and tunnels. The more general question is, how do construction companies build underground structures?
