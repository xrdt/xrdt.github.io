Books & Readings

I believe the print book is far better than the digital version. The flashing of a screen slows down your reading speed and distracts you from going into the book.

Some excuses for reading digital books is that digital books are cheaper and more portable. You could also say that reading free pdfs of books is a good way to sample the work before you decide to buy it.

I would argue that there is no need to have your entire library on your person at every moment of every day in your Kindle or other e-book reader. How often is it so absolutely necessary for you to search through your library of books for some morsel of information or for some passage? Each moment you spend reading should be one of deep concentration and immersion. Do not treat reading as some fickle, temporary activity you do to appear smart or out of necessity. When you read, you are enriching your mind by diving into the author's universe. Cherish that moment, because it is impossible to recreate.

If you want to sample books, go to your local library. If you are too poor or too miserly to buy books, use your local library. It is rare that one re-references books, anyway. There are few books in this world that are worth owning. Save your money for those books.

  • Elegant Objects, Vol 1 and 2, by Yegor Bugayenko
